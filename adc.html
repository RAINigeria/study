BLOCK 01

ADC / DAC & Sensors

⏱ 10 minutes — 6 min talk · 4 min exercise
Talk

The world is analogue. Computers are digital. Sensors and converters are the bridge.

ADC — Analogue-to-Digital Converter

Samples a voltage and converts it to a number your MCU can use.

Output = floor( Vin / Vref × 2n )

Arduino UNO: 10-bit ADC, Vref = 5V → 1024 steps → 1 step ≈ 4.9 mV. Quantisation error is ±½ LSB — unavoidable.

DAC — Digital-to-Analogue Converter

Reverse process: number → voltage. On Arduino, the cheapest DAC is PWM + a low-pass RC filter.

Vout = ( D / 2n ) × Vref

Accuracy & Measurement Terms — 30-second drill

TermDefinition
RangeMin to max the sensor can measure
Span (Full Scale)Max − Min
AccuracyCloseness to true value (expressed as %FS)
Precision / RepeatabilityHow consistent — tight cluster, possibly off-centre
ResolutionSmallest detectable change
Hysteresis(up reading − down reading) / span × 100%
Systematic errorConsistent bias — can be calibrated out
Random errorScatter — reduced by averaging (σ/√n)

Common sensors (hold each one up as you mention it)

Reed switch
Digital ON/OFF
Magnet closes ferromagnetic contacts. Built-in hysteresis between operate and release.
Incremental encoder
Pulse train (A, B, Z)
Count pulses for position. A/B phase = direction. Index Z = one pulse per revolution.
Absolute encoder
Parallel / serial code
Unique code for every position. No homing needed on power-up. Grey code avoids multi-bit errors.
NTC Thermistor
Analogue resistance
Resistance drops with temperature. Non-linear — needs lookup table or Steinhart-Hart equation.
Thermocouple
Analogue millivolts
Seebeck effect: two dissimilar metals → voltage ∝ ΔT. Needs cold-junction compensation (MAX6675 IC).
Hall effect
Analogue or digital
Magnetic field → voltage. Used in brushless motors, current sensors, position sensing.
LDR
Analogue resistance
Light lowers resistance. Slow response. Pair with a fixed resistor as a voltage divider.
HC-SR04 Ultrasonic
Pulse width (echo)
Time of flight. Range 2 cm–4 m. Distance = duration × 0.0343 / 2.
Exercise 1 — 4 minutes

ADC & Sensor Reading on Arduino

Wire: 10 kΩ potentiometer across 5V–GND, wiper to A0.

void setup() { Serial.begin(9600); }

void loop() {
  int raw = analogRead(A0);
  float v = raw * (5.0 / 1023.0);
  Serial.print("Raw: ");   Serial.print(raw);
  Serial.print("  Voltage: "); Serial.println(v, 3);
  delay(200);
}

Answer on paper:

  1. What is the LSB voltage for a 10-bit, 5 V ADC?
  2. Sweep the pot fully. What is your measured span in raw counts?
  3. Your max reading is 1018 instead of 1023 — is this systematic or random error? Why?
Challenge: If a reading is ±5 counts out of 1023, calculate the percentage accuracy error as %FS.

Smoothing & Regulation

A large electrolytic capacitor (e.g. 2200 µF) smooths the rectified pulses. Ripple voltage:

Vripple ≈ Iload / (2 × f × C)

LM7805 → stable 5V output. Simple and robust — but wastes power as heat: Ploss = (Vin − Vout) × Iload. Switching regulators (SMPS) are 90–95% efficient; used in phone chargers.

⚠ Safety: An electrolytic capacitor connected backwards will fail catastrophically. Always check polarity — the negative leg is marked with a stripe.

BLOCK 03

DC to AC — Inverters

⏱ 7 minutes — 4 min talk · 3 min exercise
Talk

Battery-powered systems sometimes need to run AC appliances. An inverter converts DC back to AC.

Inverter output waveforms

TypeWaveformCostUse case
Square wave▬▬▬LowestSimple resistive loads only (bulbs, heaters)
Modified sine waveStepped approximationMidMost appliances, some motors
Pure sine waveSmooth 50 Hz sineHighestSensitive electronics, AC motors, medical equipment

How it works

An H-bridge or full-bridge of power MOSFETs/IGBTs switches the DC supply at 50 Hz. Higher-frequency PWM shapes the waveform. An output transformer steps up to 240V. A low-pass filter removes switching harmonics.

VRMS = Vpeak / √2   →   240V RMS requires Vpeak ≈ 339V

PWM, Duty Cycle & Switching Devices

⏱ 13 minutes — 8 min talk · 5 min exercise
Talk

PWM (Pulse Width Modulation) is the single most useful technique in electronics. Master it.

100% DC
Full power
75% DC
75%
50% DC
50%
25% DC
25%
0% DC
No power
Duty Cycle = ton / T × 100%   |   Vavg = Duty Cycle × Vsupply

Arduino: analogWrite(pin, 0–255) → 0% to 100%. Pins 5 & 6 ≈ 490 Hz; pins 3, 9, 10, 11 ≈ 980 Hz.

Uses: DC motor speed, LED dimming, servo position (1–2 ms / 20 ms period), AC voltage control via TRIAC, cheap DAC.

Switching devices — pick the right one

Relay
Electromechanical. Digital coil drive. Isolates high voltage from MCU. Slow (10–15 ms). Good for mains on/off.
BJT (NPN/PNP)
Current-controlled. Base current drives collector. Loads < 1A. Cheap, fast. Use 2N2222 or TIP31.
MOSFET (N-ch)
Voltage-controlled gate. High current, low RDS loss. IRLZ44N is logic-level (5V gate). Best for DC PWM > 1A.
IGBT
Gate-voltage like MOSFET, saturates like BJT. Used in inverters, motor drives > 10A, high voltage.
Thyristor (SCR)
Latches ON after gate pulse. Turns OFF only when current drops below holding current. AC half-wave control.
TRIAC
Like two SCRs back-to-back. Controls both half-cycles of AC. AC dimming, fan speed. Use BTA16 for 16A.
Optoisolator
LED → phototransistor. Electrically isolates MCU from high voltage. Always use between Arduino and TRIAC/relay.

Arduino → Relay wiring

GPIO (5V) → [1kΩ] → Base of NPN (2N2222) Collector → Relay coil (–) Relay coil (+) → 12V supply Flyback diode (1N4007) across relay coil — banded end to +12V Arduino GND and 12V GND must share a common ground
⚠ Safety rule — no exceptions: Never connect an Arduino GPIO directly to mains. Use an optoisolator (e.g. MOC3021) → TRIAC (e.g. BTA16) → load. Use zero-crossing detection to reduce EMI.

Battery Packs, BMS & Charging

⏱ 10 minutes — 6 min talk · 4 min exercise
Talk

Specifying a pack — example: 3S2P 18650 Li-Ion

ParameterPer cell3S2P pack
Nominal voltage3.6V3 × 3.6 = 10.8V
Max voltage4.2V3 × 4.2 = 12.6V
Min voltage3.0V3 × 3.0 = 9.0V
Capacity2500 mAh2 × 2500 = 5000 mAh
Max discharge5A2 × 5 = 10A
Energy9 Wh54 Wh

BMS — Battery Management System

The BMS sits between the cells and the load/charger. It protects every cell from:

  • Overcharge — above 4.2V per cell
  • Over-discharge — below 3.0V per cell
  • Overcurrent / short circuit
  • Overtemperature

Cell balancing

Passive balancing: Burns excess charge as heat in a resistor when one cell is fuller. Simple, cheap, generates heat. Most common in hobby BMS boards.

Active balancing: Transfers charge from full cells to less-full cells. Efficient but complex and expensive. Used in EV battery packs.

Charging — TP4056 (single cell example)

  1. CC phase: Charges at constant current (e.g. 1A) until cell reaches 4.2V
  2. CV phase: Holds at 4.2V; current tapers naturally as cell fills
  3. Termination: When current drops below ~50 mA → full
C-rating: 1C = current (in A) equal to capacity (in Ah)
5 Ah pack at 1C = 5A charge/discharge rate
Multi-cell packs need dedicated balance charger ICs (e.g. BQ77915, LTC6803) or a dedicated balance charger with individual cell monitoring via the JST-XH balance connector.

BLOCK 06

Voltage Dividers

⏱ 8 minutes — 5 min talk · 3 min exercise
Talk

Two resistors in series between Vsupply and GND. The midpoint gives a fraction of the supply.

V_supply │ [ R1 ] ├────── V_out = V_supply × R2 / (R1 + R2) [ R2 ] │ GND

Classic uses

  • ADC input scaling: 0–12V signal → 0–5V for Arduino. Use R1 = 7kΩ, R2 = 3kΩ → Vout = Vin × 3/10
  • Sensor biasing: NTC thermistor as R1 + fixed R2 → temperature-dependent voltage at the midpoint
  • Logic level shifting: 5V → 3.3V (for simple unidirectional signals)
  • Op-amp reference: Set the non-inverting input voltage

The loading problem

A divider is only accurate when Rload ≫ R2. If the load draws significant current, Vout drops below the calculated value.

Loaded Vout = Vsupply × (R2 ∥ Rload) / (R1 + R2 ∥ Rload)
R2 ∥ Rload = (R2 × Rload) / (R2 + Rload)

Rule of thumb: Rload ≥ 10 × R2 for less than 10% loading error.

Exercise 6 — 3 minutes

BLOCK 07

Motor Drivers — L298N, H-Bridge, Op-Amps

⏱ 12 minutes — 7 min talk · 5 min exercise
Talk

Why you can't drive a motor directly from Arduino GPIO: GPIO pin max = 40 mA. A small DC motor needs 200 mA–2A. An inductive load also generates voltage spikes that destroy the MCU.

H-Bridge — the concept

+V │ [S1] [S2] ├─── MOTOR ───┤ [S3] [S4] │ GND S1 + S4 ON → Forward S2 + S3 ON → Reverse S1 + S2 ON → Short circuit! ✗ Never

L298N Dual H-Bridge Module

  • Controls 2 DC motors or 1 stepper motor
  • Supply: 5–35V, up to 2A per channel (4A peak)
  • Logic: 5V compatible (IN1, IN2, IN3, IN4 for direction)
  • ENA / ENB pins accept PWM for speed control
  • Has built-in flyback diodes (add external for > 1A motors)
Arduino → L298N: IN1 = D2, IN2 = D3 (direction channel A) ENA = D9 (PWM speed) Motor A wires → OUT1 / OUT2 12V supply → VS, GND → common GND

Stepper motors with L298N

Step through IN1/IN2/IN3/IN4 in sequence. Full-step pattern (hex): 1000 → 1100 → 0100 → 0110 → 0010 → 0011 → 0001 → 1001 → repeat.

Op-amps in motor circuits

ConfigurationFormulaUse in motor control
ComparatorV+ > V− → HIGHOvercurrent detection, speed threshold switching
Non-inverting ampGain = 1 + R2/R1Amplify small sensor signals (current sense, encoders)
Inverting ampGain = −R2/R1Error amplifier in PID feedback loop

Key capacitors on the bench

  • 100 nF ceramic across motor terminals → suppresses EMI / brush noise spikes
  • 100 µF electrolytic on power rail near driver IC → decouples supply, prevents voltage dips on motor start

BLOCK 10

Signals — Ultrasonic, IR, Microwave, RF

⏱ 8 minutes — 5 min talk · 3 min exercise
Talk

Ultrasonic — HC-SR04

  • Frequency: 40 kHz (inaudible). Range: 2 cm – 4 m, ±3 mm accuracy.
  • Time of flight: send 10 µs trigger pulse → measure echo return time
Distance = pulse_duration (µs) × 0.0343 / 2   (cm)

Limitation: soft/angled surfaces absorb or scatter the beam. Speed of sound changes ~0.6 m/s per °C — affects accuracy at temperature extremes.

IR — Infrared

  • Proximity: GP2Y0A21, TCRT5000 — reflected IR → distance or surface detection
  • Remote control: Modulated at 38 kHz carrier (NEC, RC5 protocols). Use TSOP38238 receiver IC.
  • Line following: IR emitter + detector pair detects black/white contrast on floor
  • Limitation: direct sunlight swamps IR sensors. Black surfaces absorb — reduce range significantly.

Microwave / Radar

e.g. HLK-LD2410 (24 GHz FMCW radar). Can detect through walls and glass. Presence detection, not confused by non-moving objects like PIR. Used in automatic doors, speed cameras, smart lighting.

Exercise 10 — 3 minutes

HC-SR04 Distance Sensor

Wire: VCC → 5V, GND → GND, Trig → D7, Echo → D8

#define TRIG 7
#define ECHO 8

void setup() {
  Serial.begin(9600);
  pinMode(TRIG, OUTPUT); pinMode(ECHO, INPUT);
}

void loop() {
  digitalWrite(TRIG, LOW);  delayMicroseconds(2);
  digitalWrite(TRIG, HIGH); delayMicroseconds(10);
  digitalWrite(TRIG, LOW);
  long dur = pulseIn(ECHO, HIGH);
  float dist = dur * 0.0343 / 2.0;
  Serial.print("Distance: "); Serial.print(dist); Serial.println(" cm");
  delay(200);
}
  1. Where does the constant 0.0343 come from?
  2. Place your hand at 20 cm. Is the reading accurate? List two possible sources of error.

WRAP-UP

10 Things to Take Away

01
ADC / DAC: every physical measurement enters the digital world through a converter. Bits = resolution. Nyquist = minimum sampling rate.
02
AC→DC needs a rectifier + smoothing cap + regulator. Linear regulators (LM78xx) waste power as heat — P = (Vin − Vout) × I.
03
PWM is universal: motor speed, LED dimming, servo position, AC voltage control, cheap DAC. Duty cycle = t_on / T × 100%.
04
Never connect a microcontroller directly to mains. Always use an optoisolator + TRIAC. One mistake destroys both the MCU and possibly you.
05
BMS is not optional for Li-Ion packs. It prevents overcharge, over-discharge, and short circuit. Balance charging extends pack lifetime significantly.
06
Voltage dividers load down. Always check that R_load ≥ 10 × R2. Otherwise your calculated Vout will be wrong under load.
07
L298N for DC motor control up to 2A. Add 100 nF ceramics across motor terminals. Move to discrete MOSFETs or BTS7960 for higher currents.
08
Simulate first, solder second. Wokwi, Falstad, and KiCad are free. A 10-minute simulation saves a 2-hour debug session.
09
Signal choice: Ultrasonic for accurate ranging, IR for proximity and line following, LoRa for long-range IoT, ESP32 for WiFi/cloud, BLE for phone app control.
10
Colour code everything. Red = +, Black = −, Green/Yellow = Earth. Label every connector. One unlabelled wire will cause a short circuit on the worst possible day.
